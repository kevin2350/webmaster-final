SPIRE Offerings



SPIRE utilizes project-based, technology-enhanced instruction to teach through an engineering lens. Students have the opportunity to work hands-on with engineering concepts through collaboration. The program has been awarded the STEM certification by the Georgia Department of Education. Ridge currently offers two different SPIRE pathways that students can follow throughout their high school years. These classes integrate engineering into general science & math classes.








"These graduates may become involved in STEM careers as a result of the increased collaboration and professional development within our school focused on increasing relevancy in the classroom as we prepare the next generation of leaders. STEM is not 'a part' of what we do 'for some of our students'. It is a tool to increase our graduate's options as well as “seal the leaks” in the STEM pipeline. Daily, we work to decrease variability between our classrooms and embrace the evolution of relevance and rigor for all of our students." - Dr. Jeff Mathews, Principal.

Career, Technology, Education

CTE Pathways



Students have the opportunity to pursue a variety of CTE pathways, with each specializing in a certain career pathway. From Transporation to Business Management, these pathways are designed to introduce and encourage technology-oriented thinking. Certain CTE pathways are application-based, where students work hands-on with real life applications and programs.

The teachers that teach these classes are certified in a specific technical field. Students that finish these pathways will have the chance to take a End of Pathway Assessment. These assessments are an industry recognized certification. Each pathway also has a certain club related to it. Much of the recruitment for these clubs come from students in the related CTE pathway.

Work Based Learning

Students looking for more hands-on and onsite learning can participate in Work Based Learning. These sessions act as independent classes, and provide course credit. Students in Work Based Learning can apply their knowledge from CTE pathways to a job.










Off-Campus Opportunities




Grayson High School of Technology

Students have the opportunity to take afternoon classes at Grayson High School of Technology. These classes focus on a specialized fields, such as Carpentry or Welding. Similar to CTE pathways at Peachtree Ridge, classes at Grayson also follow pathways with an End of Pathway Assessment that awards students with industry recognized certificates.



Maxwell High School of Technology

Students also have the opportunity to take either morning or afternoon classes at Maxwell High School of Technology. Classes are similar to those offered at Grayson, with each specializing in a certain technical field. Classes at Maxwell are in 3-hour blocks of time.

Advanced Placement

AP Classes

Ridge offers an array of college-level AP classes. These classes are taught by teachers with additional training and certification. Currently, 30 different AP classes are offered. Last year, 1,155 students at Ridge took an AP exam, and 77% earned a 3 or higher. Many of these classes are STEM-oriented.


Class Description
AP Biology Advanced labs, analyze & explain the "whys" of biological science.
AP Calculus AB / BC Covers limits, derivatives, and integrals of algebraic & transcendental functions.
AP Chemistry College-level chemistry course work, utilizes advanced lab activities.
AP Computer Science A Study of structured programming in Java & advanced programming analysis.
AP Environmental Science Covers ecology, energy resources & conservation, pollution, and biotechnology.
AP Physics 1 / C Study of matter in motion & mechanics. Physics C offers in-depth calculus-based problem solving.
AP Statistics In-depth experience in statistical concepts & methods.
AP Computer Science Principles Explore the concepts of computer science and learn the basics of programming.
